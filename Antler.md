Below is a **practical Autoresearch Success Criteria rubric** for evaluating whether a startup **wedge** is likely fundable by Antler. This is **not Antler’s official IC scorecard**; it is synthesized from Antler’s public guidance on pre-seed validation, problem-solution fit, founder quality, traction, market size, and pitch criteria.

## **Core Antler logic**

Antler invests very early — often at “inception stage” / Day Zero — but still wants **validation**, not just vision. Their public pre-seed guidance says founders need evidence across founder-market fit, technology, users, market size, use of funds, competitive landscape, and a coherent strategy. They also explicitly frame early fundability as mostly driven by **founder pedigree \+ traction**, with traction increasingly compensating for weaker pedigree. ([Antler](https://www.antler.co/blog/how-to-raise-pre-seed-round))

## **Autoresearch Success Criteria: Antler-fundable wedge**

| Criterion | Weight | Strong pass | Minimum pass | Fail |
| ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- |
| **1\. Painful problem in narrow beachhead** | 15 | Clear ICP \+ urgent problem; buyer already spends money/time solving it; problem is repeated weekly/monthly | 10+ customer interviews show strong pain | Problem is vague, “nice to have,” or broad horizontal AI wrapper |
| **2\. Specific wedge, not generic TAM** | 12 | Narrow segment with clear why-now, distribution path, and expansion logic | Beachhead is defined by role, workflow, geography, or event trigger | “Huge market” claim without first-entry segment |
| **3\. Founder-market fit** | 15 | Founder has direct lived/domain expertise, network, unfair access, or prior company-building proof | Founder can credibly explain why they are unusually suited | No clear reason this founder wins |
| **4\. Evidence of demand** | 18 | 3–5 design partners, pilots, LOIs, paid trials, or strong waitlist growth | 20–30 discovery calls \+ 5+ users willing to test | Only synthetic research or friends saying “cool idea” |
| **5\. Product proof / MVP feasibility** | 10 | Working prototype, demo, concierge MVP, or technical proof | Low-fidelity prototype or manual workflow proving outcome | Pure slideware unless deep-tech requiring funding before MVP |
| **6\. Market size \+ expansion path** | 10 | Beachhead can expand into $1B+ venture-scale market; clear adjacency map | Plausible $100M+ beachhead and larger adjacent market | Niche cannot scale or expansion is hand-wavy |
| **7\. Differentiation / defensibility** | 8 | Proprietary workflow, data, distribution, protocol advantage, or operational wedge | Clear reason incumbents do not already solve it | “We use AI” as only differentiation |
| **8\. Business model \+ unit economics** | 6 | Clear payer, pricing, gross margin logic, CAC channel, expansion revenue | Plausible pricing and buyer identified | User loves it but payer unclear |
| **9\. Timing / market trend** | 4 | Strong why-now: regulation, platform shift, protocol shift, budget shift, behavior shift | Some external tailwind | No timing reason |
| **10\. Fundraise readiness / story clarity** | 2 | Crisp one-pager: metrics, ask, use of funds, next milestone | Basic deck/memo exists | Cannot explain business in 30 seconds |

**Score interpretation**

| Score | Autoresearch output |
| ----- | ----- |
| **85–100** | Strong Antler-fundable wedge |
| **70–84** | Potentially fundable; needs sharper proof or story |
| **55–69** | Interesting but under-validated |
| **\<55** | Not fundable yet |

## **Hard gates**

For Antler-style pre-seed funding, I would require these as **mandatory gates**:

1. **Founder-market fit:** clear reason this founder/team has unfair advantage. Antler’s materials emphasize that investors need proof the founder is the right person to build the company. ([Antler](https://www.antler.co/blog/how-to-raise-pre-seed-round))  
2. **Problem-solution fit:** a deep customer problem in an underserved market, tested with a small set of early evangelists. ([Antler](https://www.antler.co/blog/problem-solution-fit))  
3. **Validation beyond vision:** at least one of: paying customer, pilot, LOI, design partner, fast-growing waitlist, or strong qualitative \+ quantitative customer proof. Antler explicitly says “vision is not enough” and that founders need validation before raising. ([Antler](https://www.antler.co/blog/how-to-raise-pre-seed-round))  
4. **Scalable venture outcome:** attractive TAM, scalable technology, market trend/timing, demonstrated traction, product-market direction, and attractive forecast unit economics. ([Antler](https://www.antler.co/blog/what-investors-look-for-in-startup-pitch))  
5. **Compelling story:** strong team \+ clear investment opportunity \+ ambition for 5–7 year venture return. ([Antler](https://www.antler.co/blog/pre-seed-pitch-deck))

## **Quantified evidence thresholds for Autoresearch**

Use these as scoring targets:

| Evidence type | Weak | Minimum | Strong |
| ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- |
| Customer discovery interviews | \<10 | 20–30 | 50+ |
| ICP specificity | Generic persona | One role / use case | One role \+ trigger \+ budget owner |
| Design partners | 0 | 2–3 | 5+ |
| LOIs / pilot commitments | 0 | 1–2 | 3+ |
| Paid customers | 0 | 1 | 3–10 |
| Waitlist | \<100 | 500+ with source quality | 2,000+ or 20%+ weekly growth |
| Prototype | None | Clickable / concierge demo | Working MVP with usage |
| User retention signal | None | Repeat usage by early testers | 30%+ weekly active return among testers |
| Willingness to pay | Hypothetical | Buyer names price range | Signed pilot / paid trial |
| Sales cycle evidence | Unknown | Buyer process mapped | Known buyer, budget, procurement path |
| Market size | “Big market” | $100M+ beachhead | $1B+ expansion path |
| Differentiation | AI wrapper | Workflow-specific advantage | Data/protocol/distribution moat |

## **One-line Success Criteria for Autoresearch**

**A wedge is Antler-fundable if it shows a narrow urgent beachhead, founder-market fit, early demand evidence, credible MVP feasibility, and a believable path from the first 10–100 customers to a venture-scale market.**
