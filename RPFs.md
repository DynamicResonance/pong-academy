# **Recent RFPs / RFS — YC \+ Robotics-Focused VCs (Nov 2025 – May 2026\)**

Seeding list for Autoresearch. Sources: YC Summer 2026 RFS (published Apr 2026), a16z American Dynamism "Big Ideas 2026" (Dec 2025), and robotics-VC theses/funding data (Dec 2025–May 2026).

## **A. YC Summer 2026 RFS — robotics/physical-AI relevant items**

YC published 15 requests. The Summer 2026 edition is more specific and technically ambitious, framed not as trends but as "gaps"—problems now solvable because AI, robotics, and biology crossed a threshold. The most robotics-adjacent:

**1\. AI for Low-Pesticide Agriculture** (Garry Tan, personally) — AI can now identify individual weeds/pests in real time; cheap sensors/cameras at scale; robotics can treat one plant instead of blanket-spraying. Quantified target: cut pesticide use by 90% while growing more food \= "a generational company." (One YC-W26 example, Synphony, cites strawberry farming as a $3B industry in California alone with 60% labor cost.)

**5\. Counter-Swarm Defense** (Tyler Bosmeny) — A Patriot missile costs $3M; an FPV drone costs $500—all cost advantage sits with attackers. Wants high-capacity interceptors, sensor-fusion software, non-kinetic defenses, and attacks on the autonomy stack; drone defense looks less like operating a weapon and more like running a real-time distributed system—winners look more like Cloudflare than Raytheon.

**7\. Electronics in Space** (talent-targeted at SpaceX/NVIDIA chip designers) — reusable rockets create surplus launch capacity; the bottleneck will be compute—inference chips optimized for mass, thermal, and radiation, for a market that barely exists yet but is about to explode.

**8\. Hardware Supply Chain** (Nicolas Dessaigne) — In Shenzhen a team goes from design to a new physical part in one day; in the US the same loop takes weeks. Wants startups producing parts dramatically faster and tightly integrating design, manufacturing, and logistics. YC-W26 examples named: Hlabs (actuators), Prototyping.io.

**9\. Industrial Capabilities in Space** (Adi Oltean) — the moon has silicon, aluminum, iron, titanium; electrolysis works better in low gravity; 3D printing from molten regolith is more efficient. Explicitly a decade-long bet.

**10\. Inference Chips for Agent Workflows** (Diana Hu) — current GPUs hit only 30–40% of peak utilization on agent workloads because the work is bursty. Wants chips for fast context switching, native speculative decoding, and memory for KV caches that persist across an execution graph. Signal: NVIDIA bought Groq for $20B because it saw this coming.

**14\. Supply Chain 2.0 for Semiconductors** (Diana Hu) — a single advanced chip goes through 1,400 process steps, crosses a dozen countries, takes five months; managed with spreadsheets, SAP, and phone calls. Quantified pain: in 2021 a $300 chip held up a $50,000 car and $210B in vehicles didn't get built. Wants real-time allocation tracking, multi-tier risk monitoring, export-compliance tooling.

(Non-physical but adjacent for autoresearch seeding: **2\. AI-Native Service Companies**, **3\. AI Personalized Medicine**, **4/15. Company Brain / AI OS for Companies**, **12\. Software for Agents**.)

## **B. a16z American Dynamism — "Big Ideas 2026" (Dec 2025\)**

Backed by a dedicated $600M fund for early-stage teams building AI for the physical world, within a16z's $15B 2026 raise (American Dynamism allocation \~$1.176B).

**AI-native industrial base** (David Ulevitch) — opportunities in advanced energy systems, robotics-heavy manufacturing, next-gen mining, and biological/enzymatic processes for precursor chemicals; AI coordinating fleets of autonomous machines.

**Renaissance of the American factory** (Erin Price-Wright) — apply a "factory mindset" to energy, mining, construction, manufacturing: modular deployment of AI and autonomy alongside skilled workers; navigate regulation/permitting, speed design cycles with design-for-manufacture, deploy autonomy for dangerous tasks—to mass-produce nuclear reactors, housing, and datacenters.

**Physical observability** (Zabie Elmgren) — more than a billion networked cameras and sensors already deployed across U.S. cities; wants privacy-preserving, interoperable, AI-native systems—a common fabric that renders the physical world as observable as code, framed as the enabling layer for the next frontier of robotics/autonomy.

**The electro-industrial stack** (Ryan McEntush) — combined technologies powering EVs, drones, data centers, and modern manufacturing: minerals → components, energy → batteries, electricity → power electronics, motion → precision motors, coordinated by software.

**Autonomous labs** (Oliver Hsu) — as robotic manipulation improves, teams will build lights-out labs that close the loop on scientific discovery from hypothesis to experiment execution. (Directly relevant to an Autoresearch product.)

**Data crusade in critical industries** (Will Bitsky) — 2026 is defined by data constraints. Each truck roll, meter read, maintenance job, production run is fodder for training, but capture/annotation/training aren't in the industrial lexicon. Startup opening: the coordination stack—collection/annotation/consent software, sensor hardware and SDKs, RL environments and training pipelines, and eventually intelligent machines.
